Searching for turbulence models by artificial neural network

Authors: Masataka Gamahara, Yuji Hattori

Affiliation: Masataka Gamahara: Graduate School of Information Sciences, Tohoku University; Yuji Hattori: Institute of Fluid Science, Tohoku University

DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevFluids.2.054604
Journal: PHYSICAL REVIEW FLUIDS, Volume 2, Pages 054604-1 - 054604-20, Year 2017

Abstract:
An artificial neural network (ANN) is tested as a tool for finding a new subgrid model of the subgrid-scale (SGS) stress in large-eddy simulation. An ANN is used to establish a functional relation between the grid-scale flow field and the SGS stress without any assumption of the form of function. Data required for training and test of the ANN are provided by direct numerical simulation of a turbulent channel flow. It is shown that an ANN can establish a model similar to the gradient model. The correlation coefficients between the real SGS stress and the output of the ANN are comparable to or larger than similarity models, but smaller than a two-parameter dynamic mixed model. Large-eddy simulations using the trained ANN are also performed. Although ANN models show no advantage over the Smagorinsky model, the results confirm that the ANN is a promising tool for establishing a new subgrid model with further improvement.

Research Points

  • An artificial neural network (ANN) is proposed as a tool to establish a new subgrid-scale (SGS) model for large-eddy simulation (LES) of turbulence.
  • The ANN is trained and tested using data from direct numerical simulation (DNS) of turbulent channel flow to find a functional relationship between the grid-scale flow field and the SGS stress.
  • The performance of the ANN-based model is compared with existing models like the Smagorinsky model and the gradient model.

Main Findings

  • The ANN is capable of creating a model similar to the gradient model, with high correlation coefficients between the predicted and actual SGS stress.
  • The ANN-based model shows correlation coefficients comparable to or larger than similarity models, but smaller than a two-parameter dynamic mixed model.
  • While the a posteriori tests show that the ANN model has no significant advantage over the Smagorinsky model in predicting the mean flow, it demonstrates that ANNs are a promising approach for developing new turbulence models.

Conclusion

An ANN can successfully establish a functional relationship between the grid-scale flow field and the subgrid-scale (SGS) stress tensor in LES. The model created by the ANN is found to be similar to the gradient model and shows good correlation with DNS data, but does not offer a significant advantage over the conventional Smagorinsky model in a posteriori tests. The study concludes that using ANNs is a promising methodology for discovering new and improved turbulence models, although further improvements are needed.
